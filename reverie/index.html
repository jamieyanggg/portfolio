Reverie

A personalized mindfulness app designed to help users manage stress and build lasting self-care habits through emotional resonance and daily ritual.

TypeMobile App
RoleProduct Designer
Collaborators1 UI/UX Designer
Timeline3 Weeks
01 Overview

About the Project

Reverie is a mobile donation experience designed around calm, cosmic aesthetics and emotional gratification. Rather than presenting charity as obligation, Reverie reframes giving as a ritual of hope, self-forgiveness, and daily completion.

02 Problem Statement

Why existing platforms fall short

Gen Z and Millennials want to advocate for causes and feel impactful, but existing donation platforms feel transactional, untrustworthy, or emotionally distant.

They need a simple, aesthetically pleasing, emotionally resonant system that transforms small daily actions into meaningful rituals — without feeling financially burdensome or manipulative.

03 Objectives

What we set out to do

01

Simplify donation into low-friction daily actions

Remove cognitive load by creating a one-tap ritual that integrates into morning or evening routines naturally.

02

Make users feel impactful and validated

Design emotional feedback loops — progress, gratitude, and impact visualization — that make small contributions feel significant.

03

Build habitual engagement through aesthetics

Leverage cosmic, calm visual language to differentiate Reverie from utilitarian competitors and drive daily return visits.

06 User Research

4 interviews, 3 key themes

We spoke with 4 college students who care about social causes but struggle to give consistently.

"I don't know where to start."

→ Users felt overwhelmed by too many choices. Curation and mood-matching emerged as a primary need.

"I need something quick."

→ Most preferred 5–10 minute sessions. Speed and simplicity were non-negotiable for busy students.

"I forget to stay consistent."

→ Lack of gentle reminders and streaks reduced long-term engagement across all participants.

What users said they needed most

Simplicity / low effort100%
Emotional reward after giving75%
Seeing impact in real time75%
Reminder / habit nudge50%
Social sharing25%

09 Iterations

How the design evolved

Usability testing revealed the need to simplify navigation and make mood-based recommendations more prominent.

v1 → v2

Removed bottom navigation bar with 5 tabs. Users were confused — consolidated into 3 core actions.

v2 → v3

Moved mood-based recommendations from settings to the home screen front and center.

v3 → Final

Added streak visualization after testing showed it was the #1 driver of return visits among participants.

10 Usability Testing

Validation & findings

🧭 Navigation Clarity

80% of participants preferred the simplified bottom navigation with only 3 key actions vs the original 5-tab bar.

🎭 Mood Personalization

Users engaged 2× more when content adapted to their stated emotional state at session start.

⏱ Session Length

Short sessions under 10 minutes increased daily retention — 3-day streak visibility was the top motivator.
